Celsius to Fahrenheit To convert a temperature from
Celsius to Fahrenheit and viz, formulae:
F=C × 9/5 + 32
C=(F-32)×5/9
pracical example:
Travel and Weather Reports
Scenario: You're traveling from the United States
(where Fahrenheit is commonly used) to Europe (where Celsius is commonly used).
Use Case: You see a weather report in Europe that says
the temperature is 20°C. To understand this in terms
you're familiar with, you convert it to Fahrenheit.
F=20 × 9/5 + 32 = 68°F Conclusion: You know it's a comfortable 68°F, which helps you decide what clothes to pack. dev TS Mthimuunye @ thandolwakhemthimunye@gmail.com
